# Tidal Minimalism — icon design philosophy

A single confident silhouette on a calm gradient sea. No outlines, no ornament:
the subject is cut from pure form and reads instantly at 1 cm. Every curve is a
deliberate bezier, supersampled and resolved so edges are glass-smooth — the work
of patient refinement, not a quick trace.

**Form & space.** One protagonist, centered, breathing in generous negative
space. The dolphin arcs as if mid-leap: a long rostrum, a hooked dorsal, a sleek
flank tapering to horizontal flukes. Recognizable by gesture alone.

**Colour & material.** A two-stop vertical gradient — light above, deeper below —
evoking water catching the light. The subject is a single tone that flips with the
system: white on cyan for light, luminous cyan on near-black for dark, neutral grey
for the tint mode. The OS lays Liquid Glass over the result.

**Craft.** Each appearance is rendered at 4× and downsampled, so no jagged step
survives. Master-level execution: the silhouette must look labored-over, the
product of deep care, indistinguishable from a studio mark.
